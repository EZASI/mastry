Chapter I — Origin

One island.
One tree.
One resin the world
has chased for
five millennia.

Mastiha grows nowhere else on earth but the sun-cracked southern groves of Chios. Each summer the bark is scored by hand, and the tree weeps — slow, crystalline tears of aromatic resin. We press these tears into a single pure essential oil, then let them dissolve into the mineral-quiet spring water of Japan.

Chapter II — The Ritual

Not a drink. A pause.

MASTRY is built for the moment after — after the heat, after the day, after the noise. Served cold, it asks for nothing but attention.

01

Chill

六度

Serve at 6–8°C. Cold tempers the resin and lets the forest rise slowly, never sharp.

02

Open

一滴

The first note is woody and clean — the tree. Then citrus, then a long mineral finish that lingers like breath.

03

Arrive

ととのい

Drink slowly. This is ととのい — the quiet equilibrium the Japanese chase, and the calm the Aegean has always known.
