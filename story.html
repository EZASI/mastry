Chapter II — Origin

One island.
One tree.
One resin the world
has chased for
five millennia.

Mastiha grows nowhere else on earth but a few sun-warmed groves in the south of Chios. For five thousand years it has been prized to clear the head and settle the body — the Mediterranean's oldest taste of calm. Each summer the bark is scored gently by hand, and the tree weeps slow, crystalline tears of aromatic resin.

Chapter IV — Craft

Rare by
nature.

A single kilogram of mastiha oil takes a season of hand-scored bark to gather — so concentrated that one kilogram scents thirty thousand bottles. Its character is intense; Japan's soft spring water is what carries it. Low in minerals, the water rounds the resin's edge and lets it open gently, never sharp. We add nothing else: no sugar, no sweetener, no preservative. Only resin, water, and air.
